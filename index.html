About Me

Phuong-Duy Nguyen (/New-wen Foong U-e) a.k.a Jay, a Vietnamese researcher and consultant, who has over six years of experience working in the field of environmental economics and urban studies. He has worked as a research assistant at Can Tho University, and a project specialist at Vietnam Chamber of Commerce and Industry. Most recently, he is a researcher at the Can Tho City Institute, leading projects on urban governance, sustainability and circular economy. Throughout his career, Duy has directly contributed to several impactful projects such as the Mekong Delta Resilient Business Network Initiative supporting businesses in adapting to climate change; Studies on the role of informal sector in waste management in the Mekong Delta; Assessments of urban governance metrics (e.g., Competitiveness Index, City Prosperity Index) aimed at improving public service delivery and sustainable urban development; and, (pre-)feasibiltity studies on development issues (e.g., the plastic pond liner conversion schemes and sustainable urban cooling solutions).
